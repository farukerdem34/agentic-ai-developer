### Day 1

#### What is SDLC?

The software development lifecycle (SDLC) is the cost-effective and time-efficient process that development teams use to design and build high-quality software.

##### Core Phrases of SDLC

###### Plan 

Cost-benefit analysis, scheduling, resource estimation, and allocation are common tasks included in the planning phase. To build a software requirement specification document, the development team gathers requirements from a variety of stakeholders, including managers, internal and external specialists, and customers.

###### Implement

The development team codes the product during the implementation stage. They examine the specifications to find smaller coding jobs they can complete on a regular basis to produce the desired outcome.

###### Test

To find faults in the software, the development team uses both automated and manual testing. Testing the software for flaws and determining whether it satisfies client requirements are two aspects of quality analysis. The testing phase frequently occurs concurrently with the development phase since many teams test the code they write right away.

###### Deploy

Teams who develop software utilize a separate copy of the program for testing and coding than the one that users have access to. Other copies are referred to as being in the build environment or testing environment, whilst the software used by customers is referred to as production.

###### Maintain

The team handles software modifications, repairs problems, and answers customer issues throughout the maintenance phase. In order to find fresh approaches to enhance the current software, the team also keeps an eye on overall system performance, security, and user experience.

#### Why SDLC Matters?

Here are some benefits of SDLC:

- Increased visibility of the development process for all stakeholders involved
- Efficient estimation, planning, and scheduling
- Improved risk management and cost estimation
- Systematic software delivery and better customer satisfaction
