### Day 5

#### Stakeholder Questions

|Number|Question|
|-|-|
|1|When you share a video to the app, what exact details do you expect to be automatically extracted?|
|2|What happens if the creator didn't write the recipe in the caption, but only put text overlays in the video?|
|3|How long are you willing to wait on the "saving..." screen before you get frustrated and close the app?|
|4|Once the recipe is saved, how do you naturally want to look for it later?|
|5|Do you need to edit the recipe after the app extracts it, in case it missed a measurement?|
|6|Should the app notify you if a saved video is later deleted by the original creator on Instagram/TikTok?|
|7|Do you want to be able to access your saved recipes completely offline when you are at the grocery store?|
|8|What should the app do if you accidentally share a non-food post (like a funny meme) to it?|
|9|Would you ever want to share a cleanly formatted recipe from this app with someone else?|
|10|Do you prefer seeing a grid of thumbnail images for your recipes, or a text-based list of titles?|

#### Problem vs Solution

|Problem|
|-|
|Finding a recipe I saved last week on Instagram takes too long because the native 'Saved' folder is a chaotic mix of food, memes, and clothes.|

|Solution|
|-|
|We need to build a complex, AI-driven machine learning model that automatically scans the user's Instagram account, identifies food images, and auto-tags them.|

|Why It's Premature|
|-|
|It jumps immediately to an expensive, heavy technical fix without validating if a simpler solution—like a focused, manual share-extension app that just parses text—solves the core organization issue perfectly well.|

#### Constraints

|Constraint||
|-|-|
|Business|Operational, hosting, and development costs must be kept to an absolute minimum.|
|Time|Limited development resources require a strictly scoped MVP (v1.0) to ensure a timely release and avoid feature creep.|
|Legal|Data extraction must strictly comply with third-party Terms of Service (e.g., Instagram/TikTok policies) and standard data privacy laws.|
|Technical|Centralized, low-latency logging is required to quickly detect and fix parsers that break when social media platforms update their UI structures.|


#### Ambiguity Hunt

|Vague Request|
|-|
|Make the recipe extraction better|

|Clarifying Questions|
|-|
|Do you need more precise measurement extraction, or cleaner visual formatting of the text?|
|Is the current extraction failing on a specific platform, or across all of them?|
|Does "better" mean a faster processing time or higher data accuracy?|
|Can you provide a specific URL where the extraction produced incorrect results?|
